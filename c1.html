Architecture and engineering

What is an architect?
is the art and discipline of creating a building, room or other environment that people use for an intended purpose. An architect plans, designs and directs the construction of those environments. There are many types of architects. Some have a broader scope, while others involve a narrower skill set. No matter what field you choose, you can ultimately apply a range of engineering, technological and design principles to complete your tasks. Many jobs in architecture require a minimum of a bachelor's degree from an accredited program. Depending on the state, architects may need to complete a licensing process to become registered architects through the National Architectural Accrediting Board (NAAB). Several different types of architects include:


1. Audiovisual architect
Audiovisual architects or engineers focus on custom audio and visual entertainment systems. Those in this role may collaborate with building architects and structural designers to design, build and install audiovisual equipment in commercial, industrial and residential settings. They typically hold at least a bachelor's degree in architecture or a related field.

2. Building architect
A building architect designs and plans out the elements of buildings. They work in a variety of settings, including government sectors, public organizations and private firms. Building architects typically design private and public dwellings, complexes, educational and manufacturing facilities and even commercial plazas. They typically hold at least a bachelor's degree in architecture or a related field.

3. Commercial architect
A commercial architect designs and develops commercial spaces like office buildings and retail spaces. For example, they may work for an architectural firm or a business specializing in real estate management. They design projects such as shopping malls, schools, universities or apartment complexes that consider the movement of people and the purpose of the building. Commercial architects draw the designs, choose the materials and present them to the clients.

4. Restoration architect
Conservation or restoration architects focus on repairing and restoring older buildings and other structures to resemble their original presentation. They often collaborate with restoration projects to help preserve buildings. They conduct research and first-hand inspections to understand the historical value and significance of the building being restored.

5. Data warehouse architect
A data warehouse architect is in charge of planning and designing a data warehouse for private corporations, government entities and nonprofit organizations. These computer science employees use a combination of advanced technical skills and architectural design expertise to plan out data warehousing technologies to support businesses' and organizations' data storage and security needs. They typically hold at least a bachelor's degree in architecture or a related field.

6. Extreme architect
Extreme architects specialize in designing and constructing structures in extreme climates. With the rise in global warming, natural disasters have become frequent, and authorities have classified more areas as extreme zones, requiring the specialized knowledge of extreme architects.

7. Historic preservation architect
A historic preservation architect evaluates structures and preserves old buildings with architectural and historical importance. They evaluate the value of a building according to its period, material quality and condition. They can design renovations and make repairs that preserve the original design while improving the longevity of the building. A preservation architect may have traditional architecture schooling with a focus on history.

8. Industrial architect
An industrial architect designs structures for industrial use, such as factories or industrial parks. When developing a design, they consider their client's industry and the materials the client manufactures in the building. They also consider the client's needs regarding space, access and machinery.

9. Interior architect
Interior architects plan the interior rooms of buildings, including the location of walls, windows and doors. They also determine where to place pieces of furniture. Interior architects need to understand the nuances of human behavior to construct environments that best serve their clients in their daily lives. They may also develop color schemes and specify various interior finishes according to the client's wishes and the intended use of the space.

10. Landscape architect
Landscape architects design the landscapes of outdoor locations such as playgrounds, college campuses and community gardens. They often possess a keen knowledge of how to coordinate natural materials such as trees, flowers and other foliage with certain buildings and roads in the environment.
