Arts, culture and entertainment

What are creatives?
Creatives are individuals who use their talents to make something original through creative expression. Creative people practice visual and theatrical arts, written and musical expression and other creative pursuits. They are intrinsically motivated to create something new to share ideas, inspire others and entertain an audience. Creatives tend to approach work as another outlet for their creativity and are often most fulfilled when they can dedicate their lives to their art. Jobs in the creative arts Many creative professionals want a job outside of traditional business work. They desire to use their talents to entertain and inspire others through art, music, theater and other arts and entertainment pursuits. Jobs in the creative arts can be fulfilling and pay average to above average wages.


1. Illustrator
Primary duties: Illustrators are artists who create pictures to represent a writer's words. They often work together with authors to make colorful images for children's books, graphic additions to textbooks or manuals and other visuals that compliment a piece of writing.

2. Author
>Primary duties: Authors create with their words. Fiction and non-fiction authors write content sought by traditional publishers or work independently to publish and market their own writing. Anyone with a passion for words and a talent for writing can become an author by dedicating time and effort to their craft. Authors can also work in the publishing industry or as a scriptwriter for various media.

3. Video editor
Primary duties: Creative individuals with a love for film can work as a video editor, revising and changing recorded video to craft a story. Video editors can work for film projects, helping create a director's vision by arranging scenes like puzzle pieces. Video editors can also work with digital content for social media or websites.

4. Animator
Primary duties: Animators turn drawings into moving pictures. They help conceptualize characters and settings, creating a visual story through the use of traditional and digital tools. Animators may work on staff for movie and television production companies, collaborating with fellow artists to make a show. They can also work for video game producers to create game scenes and characters. Some creative professionals also complete animated projects for the business sector.

5. Choreographer
Primary duties: A choreographer uses dance to express ideas. They plan movements that sync with music to create a dance performance for entertainment. Choreographers create routines for live theater, concerts and film productions. Some choreographers also choose to teach dance to learners of all ages.

6. Graphic designer
Primary duties: Graphic designers create images for business and artistic purposes. They may be asked to design a new logo for a company or create the artwork for the cover of a book. Graphic designers use artistic foundations in color theory, visual weight and composition to create imagery.

7. Composer
Primary duties: Composers use a deep knowledge of music theory, instrumentation and vocals to create original music compositions. They can apply their talents to a range of industries in the creative arts including composing for professional musicians, entertainment productions, mobile apps, games and even ambient music for theme park attractions.

8. Video game designer
Primary duties: A video game designer uses their creativity to develop the main aspects of a video game from helping craft the story and characters to planning how users interact with each feature of the gameplay.

9. Makeup artist
Primary duties: Makeup artists use their creative artistry to enhance the beauty of a client. Using cosmetic products as their medium, makeup artists work for various industries including events, fashion and entertainment. Some professionals also choose to work in special effects makeup, creating character looks for film, movie and stage productions.

10. Art director
Primary duties: Art directors oversee the visual design of a publication, piece of media or a production. Their creative vision helps tell a story or send a message through the use of graphics, visual layout, color and font choice. On a film or stage production, the art director establishes the overall artistic theme of the sets, costumes and other stylistic choices. An art director may also choose to work in the field of advertising, using their creativity to blend artistic expression with marketing.
