Installation, repair and maintenance

What is a maintenance job?
A maintenance job is a professional role where employees keep buildings, machines, grounds or tools in good condition. This may require maintenance employees to clean, inspect, organize, preserve or perform repairs. Some maintenance professionals perform general repairs and upkeep tasks for residential or commercial properties. Other maintenance professionals specialize in inspecting, repairing and keeping specific types of machines or Tools in good condition, such as electronics, mills, industrial machinery or aircraft.


1. Industrial mechanic
An industrial mechanic evaluates, fixes and performs maintenance work on machines used in manufacturing processes or facilities. They may also work on pipelines that transport oil or other natural materials to and from refineries. Responsibilities include performing diagnostic tests on equipment to determine what fixes to make, reviewing technical manuals for information on specific machines, replacing or upgrading parts and cleaning or lubricating machinery.

2. Facilities manager
Facilities managers supervise the maintenance staff and operations on commercial properties. A facilities manager oversees both routine maintenance or repairs and larger building maintenance projects. They manage both the staff schedules and project budgets related to maintenance for their properties. Other responsibilities include keeping buildings up to code, hiring third-party vendors to perform maintenance or repair duties, making sure that their properties adhere to all local or federal health regulations and keeping records of maintenance work and payments.

3. Telecommunications equipment installers and repairers
A telecommunications equipment installer and repairer sets up, inspects, fixes and maintains systems that transport communication systems, such as internet routers and telephone wires. Also called telecom technicians, telecommunications equipment installers and repairers sometimes specialize in managing communication systems for a specific type of location, such as residential homes or distribution centers.

4. Electrical and electronics installers and repairers
Primary duties: Electrical and electronics installers and repairers set up, inspect, fix and maintain electrical systems and electronic devices. Some electrical and electronics installers and repairers specialize in repairing and conducting maintenance on a specific type of equipment, such as tools that use electric motors or machines found at generating stations. These professionals often use a range of tools to help them evaluate and fix machinery, including tools that rely on software like multimeters. An electrical and electronics installer and repairer's job duties vary depending on their specialization, but may include discussing their clients' needs, taking equipment apart to inspect pieces or replace them, collaborating with other professionals like electricians and maintaining diligent records

5. Maintenance planner
A maintenance planner supervises and organizes maintenance staff and operations at their company. Maintenance planners typically work at manufacturing businesses, but may also find employment opportunities at other large organizations with machinery or grounds that require regular upkeep. Maintenance planners perform job duties such as managing the maintenance budget, coordinating shifts for different maintenance employees and projects, generating cost estimate reports for employers and keeping track of supply inventory levels.
